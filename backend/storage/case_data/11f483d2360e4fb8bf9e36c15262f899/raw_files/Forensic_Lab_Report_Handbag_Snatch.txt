STRAP DAMAGE ANALYSIS:
─────────────────────

Damage Type:          Tear/rupture of leather strap
Location:             Approximately 3 cm from attachment point (left side)
Damage Pattern:       Linear tear, approximately 5-7 cm in length
Tear Characteristics: Jagged edges indicate sudden forceful pulling
Direction:            Horizontal tear (consistent with lateral pulling force)
Stress Analysis:      Tear shows signs of sudden tensile stress

Forensic Assessment:
The strap damage is entirely consistent with a forceful snatching attempt. The 
jagged edges and tear pattern indicate that the strap was subjected to sudden, 
violent pulling in a lateral direction. This is typical of handbag snatching 
attempts when the victim resists.

The damage indicates that significant force was applied suddenly, rather than a 
gradual weakening of the strap material. This supports the witness and victim 
accounts of a sudden, violent attack.

The location of the tear (near attachment point) is exactly where stress would 
be concentrated during a snatching attempt.


FIBER EVIDENCE ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────

Fibers Recovered:     YES - Present on torn strap area
Fiber Type:          Black synthetic fibers
Fiber Count:         12 distinct fibers recovered
Fiber Length:        3-8 mm
Fiber Color:         Black (consistent with black hoodie)
Fiber Composition:   Polyester blend (typical hoodie material)

Fiber Source Analysis:
The black synthetic fibers are consistent with a black hoodie or similar black 
synthetic garment. The fiber characteristics match common polyester hoodie 
material sold commercially.

The fibers likely transferred from the suspect's clothing during the physical 
struggle and attempted snatching. This is typical trace evidence in violent 
property crimes.


FINGERPRINT EVIDENCE ANALYSIS:
────────────────────────────────

Fingerprint Search Status: UNSUCCESSFUL
Condition:                 No usable fingerprints recovered
Reason for Failure:       Evidence contamination
Analysis:
The handbag strap and surrounding areas were examined for fingerprint evidence 
using standard enhancement techniques (ninhydrin, iodine fuming, cyanoacrylate).

However, no identifiable fingerprints were recovered. This is likely due to:
1. Contamination from multiple handlers (victim, witness, security guard, police)
2. Weather exposure (outdoor incident)
3. Leather surface material (difficult surface for fingerprint recovery)
4. Time delay between incident and evidence collection

While fingerprint evidence would have been valuable, the lack thereof does not 
undermine the strength of other evidence (fiber, CCTV, witness testimony).
